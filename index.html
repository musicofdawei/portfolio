SCAD summer program, first digital animation
I never had access to a drawing tablet before, and had held off on buying one despite how much digital art interested me because I was worried about how the feel would disrupt my usual “flow” when drawing. I had attempted digital art before with a mousepad, so that was my only experience to go off of. I found digital art with a tablet to be much more gratifying than what I originally envisioned, and I also learned a lot about animation for having essentially no experience with it in this medium. This is the end product of 12 hours of work. Made on toon boom.
Pixel animation, stick figure with magic orb
This is me beginning to teach myself sprite animation and gdot independently for a mod I want to make. This stickmen was my first real jump into trying animation and honestly it was pretty fun seeing what I could do. It's been a bit since I’ve started and I’ve been seeing some clear improvement from my beginning animations. Made on aesprite
Flipbook animation
Did this for a weeklong art class project. I chose to try and make a flipbook animation, needless to say, it really got me to appreciate hand drawn animation a lot more than i originally did. I used paper too thick to make it an actual flipbook, so this little montage will have to do. For my first attempt at something like this I'm genuinely proud of how it turned out, it looked more fluid than I could ever imagine.
3-D modeling college summer program
I learned this as part of a college summer program, and got access to use maya to create my week long project, the industry leading software. I chose to learn this to have more experience with what digital artwork is like since my experience in 3-D is minimal. While there was some initial difficulty with the UI, 3-d modeling ended up being nice to learn. I was surprised on how much I accomplished in such a short amount of time. SNAKE SNOWMAN
Unreal VR Game
Created this piece at the UNCA summer program. The main idea of the project was learning Unreal engine 5’s basic tools, but I chose to go beyond and try to add some of my own code.
Gameboy game NC State summer pre-college program
Worked at a pace to a complete product within a week, simulating a similar pace of an actual game studio though products are of much smaller scale. Emphasized group work, efficiency, and iterating on ideas, which was the focus of the whole first day. The project was open ended and everyone had to independently put in the effort. The final game was a top down rpg that was modeled like an old gameboy game, it had a fully functioning timer system, win/lose screen, multiple endings, and its own music! PLAY THE GAME with J and arrow keys.
